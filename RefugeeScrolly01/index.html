Journey of the Displaced

Over the past several years, the world has been faced with the displacement of Congolese refugees. Thousands of men, women and children have been forced from their homes in the Congo and have taken refuge overseas in the states. As of January 2014, almost half a million refugees had fled the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) making the DRC refugee population the sixth larget in the world. Refugees like, Musa, is one of the many have taken that journey in hopes of resettlement. Here is his story.

Conflict at Home

At the age of 16, Musa and his family were attacked by rebel soldiers. His dad was killed, his mother's leg was broken and his sisters were raped in front of them while inside their home.

Various conflicts since the 1960s have created Congolese refugees, and refugees from the DRC now represent 18 per cent of the total refugee population in Africa.

Arrival to a New Land

Upon arrival to the Erie, Pennslyvania, Musa spoke very little English. It was the U.S. Committee for Refugees and Immigrants that helped him find housing, a job and a close knit Congolese community.

(Graph: Amount of refugees that came in through USCRI in 2016= - Bhutan: 218, Burundi: 6, Congo: 73, Iran: 3, Somalia: 80)

A Success Story

Musa now works at the International Institute of Erie as a translator. It is his job to help new African refugees with the necessary paperwork upon their arrival. Musa can speak four to five different languages.

"The International Institute in Erie helps you figure out your new life in America. It is a sense of foundation. I'm very happy here which is why I work hard." - Musa

Hope for the Future

Musa lives with his six children, wife and mother in a comfortable home. He hopes to eventually reunite with his entire family and to continue to find peace in his new home.
